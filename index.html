Lexington by its Numbers in 2025

Lexington is mostly described through its history, its schools, Minuteman bikeway, MassAve and its coffee shops.

Behind every number here is a person who we share our town with - our neighbor, our child's classmate, a familiar voice at the town meeting, our friend and the family down the street who turns on their porch light at night.

This article is an attempt to describe Lexington through its numbers - what its population looks like, how it works, and where it lives.

Total Population Broken Down by Gender

The split between males and females is almost even close to a 50-50 balance suggesting that the town's demographic structure is healthy. This indicates a balanced mix of household and a stable school population.

Total Population broken down by age group

The largest sector belongs to people between 45 to 54 years old. This reveals a strong presence of households raising childrens. The next largest sector is that of people between 65-74 years old which suggests that people prefer to age in palce and may be long term home owners.

The 20 to 24 years old is the smallest segment suggesting that people tend to move away for education and early career opportunities probably returning later in life to establish families of their own.

Total Working Population broken down by income

The most important insight to draw from this chart is that 56% of working population earns $200k or more. Thus the majority of households are clustered at the very top of the income bracket. This economic layer is well above the national and state averages. This proportion of people earning between $150k to $200k and $100k to $150k is also sizable.

This distribution can typically correlate with high home values and a strong local economy.

Population broken down by Race

The two largest groups are by far the Whites and Asians. Whites are a reflective of historically white New England subarbs. But the size of Asian population is particularly notable - it also forms a major demographic pillar of the town.

It also states that the black representation is relatively smaller even below the state averages.

The two or more races category is also not negligible. This indicates intergenerational diversity, generational blending and an evolution beyond single category indetity.

Total Housing Units

The town primarily favours stand alone houses. This aligns strongly with home owners being long-term.

Even though there are many apartment or multi-unit dwellings, they are not very common. This suggests that entry level housing stock is constrained and the density seems to be relatively low compared to other urban areas.

Working Population broken down by Industry

Lexington is a knowledge economy town with the two largest secotrs alone forming the working economy of the town outpacing the traditional industries such as contruction, retail and manufacturing. This distribution suggests that the professional community is closely tied to Boston's innovation, healthcare and education sectors. Most people are likely to travel to downtown for work.

All these numbers taken together sketch a picture of Lexington as a prosperous, knowledge-driven community with a strong emphasis on long-term homeownership and a high standard of living.
